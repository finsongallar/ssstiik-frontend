Posting at the right time can be the difference between a video that blows up and one that goes unnoticed. TikTok distributes your content to more people in the first hours after publication, so it's crucial to post when your audience is most active.

üìä Why Does Timing Matter?

When you post a video on TikTok, the algorithm initially shows it to a small group of people. If these first viewers engage well (watch to the end, like, comment), the algorithm understands that the content is good and distributes it to more people.

üö´ Times to Avoid

  • Late night (1 AM - 5 AM): Very few people online

  • Business hours (9 AM - 11 AM): People focused on work/school

üé¨ Conclusion

Posting time is an important factor, but it's not magic. A bad video posted at the perfect time will still perform poorly. Use this information as an additional tool in your strategy, always prioritizing content quality.
